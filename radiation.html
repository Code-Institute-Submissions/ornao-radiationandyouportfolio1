What is radiation?


Radiation is energy that is all around us every minute of every day.


We are exposed to radiation in three main ways:


Sun

The sun exposes us to cosmic radiation

Flying in a plane increases our exposure to radiation from the sun as we have less protection fromthe earths atmosphere.

Your radiation exposure on a return flight from Dublin to New York is the same as the exposure from having two or three chest x-rays.

Food

Radiation, in the form of radioactivity, is found in many foods.

For example, bananas contain small amounts of a radioactive substance called Potassium-40.

This means that you are very slightly radioactive at all times!

Radon Gas

Radon gas orginates from the rocks and soil beneath our feet.

In Ireland, most of our radiation exposure comes from breathing Radon gas.

Depending on where you live, your exposure to Radon gas can go up or down.

These forms of radiation ( ) are collectively termed "Background Radiation"


On average, people living in Ireland are exposed to 4mSv of background radiation per year


(mSv is shorthand for millSievert which is how we measure radiation exposure)
