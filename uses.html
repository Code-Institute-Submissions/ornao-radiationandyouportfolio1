How do we use radiation in medicine?


Radiation is a very useful tool in medicine.


It is usually used in the form of X-RAYs or, in Nuclear Medicine, GAMMA RAYS.


Both allow us to see inside the human body. Both can help doctors assess injuries and diseases in a quick and easy manner.



There are FIVE ways that radiation is used in our hospital:

General Radiography

Most of our patients have this type of imaging.

These are typically images of our bones (e.g. hand or foot).

Computed Tomography (CT)

Fluoroscopy

Nuclear Medicine

DEXA
